Learn About Me

I build AI systems at the intersection of clinical medicine and machine learning — the kind that analyze throat images to detect infections, classify cough sounds to flag disease conditions, and process patient symptom data to support physicians in real-time diagnosis.

As an ML Engineer at CurieDx, a US healthcare startup, I work on production clinical AI pipelines deployed across multiple hospital sites. My work spans fine-tuning YOLO models for medical image object detection, prompt-engineering clinical LLMs like MedGemma for diagnostic reasoning, and building audio classification models that differentiate disease conditions from cough patterns.

I hold a B.E. in Biomedical Engineering and an M.Tech in AI & ML from Symbiosis Institute of Technology. I've published research in journals including SAGE (Journal of Endourology) and CMC, with collaborators at University of Southampton NHS, Avignon Université, and Manipal Institute of Technology. I hold two patents and have been cited 48+ times on Google Scholar.

I'm passionate about making AI practical for clinical environments — not just publishing papers about it, but building systems that physicians trust and patients benefit from. Based in Chennai, I'm available for speaking engagements, workshops, guest lectures, and research collaborations.

What I Build

Areas of Expertise

Clinical AI Pipelines

End-to-end ML systems for healthcare diagnostics — from data ingestion across multiple hospital sites to model deployment. Currently building multi-modal diagnostic pipelines combining symptom data, imaging, and audio at CurieDx.

Medical Computer Vision

Object detection and image classification for clinical imaging. Training YOLO models for automated frame selection in medical video captures, achieving 96% prediction agreement with >0.92 median IoU.

LLM Prompt Engineering for Healthcare

Designing clinical reasoning frameworks for medical LLMs. Led a systematic prompt engineering series on MedGemma for diagnostic classification, identifying the ceiling of prompt-based approaches on real patient data.

Audio-Based Disease Classification

Building classifiers that detect disease conditions from cough audio. Developed an ensemble of binary models using focal loss and domain-adapted pretraining, with production-ready performance for disease detection.
